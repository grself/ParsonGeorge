Gallery

Guests who complete my ghost tour frequently ask me to look at photos that they took during the tour and then they will later send those photos to me. This page is my way to share those photos with the world. Tap any of the images on this page to see the full-size photo.

The Courthouse is said to be the fourth most haunted spot in Tombstone and we regularly get photos of paranormal activity here.

Boothill is the most haunted Tombstone location and my guests often get great photos of paranormal activity here. This photo shows a few orbs over the graveyard.

Heath's Corner, at Second and Toughnut, is the eighth most haunted location in Tombstone. This photo shows a nice plasma at that corner.

A vortex is the second stage of an apparition's materialization process. This photo shows a vortex at Boothill Graveyard.

Allen Street Cemetery has been open since 1884 and is Tombstone's current cemetery. Not many guests visit this site but I occasionally get a few photos from here.

The Birdcage Theatre is the third most haunted spot in Tombstone and this photo shows what appears to be the top half of a face peeking over the window ledge.

These are orbs in the street beside our city park. You can see the entrance to the OK Corral in the background.

This shows a vortex at the Courthouse. If this apparition continues through all four stages then it will eventually materialize near where this vortex will be touching the ground.

A plasma is the third stage of a materialization and the bottom right corner of this photo captured a good example. Some people think that this resembles a man walking, but it's too soon in the process to know for sure.

This is a clearly-defined vortex at Boothill Graveyard. Most vortexes are somewhat vertical but this has an odd horizontal orientation.

This is not an apparition, it's Lee McKechnie, the co-owner and occasional driver of the Ghost and Murder Tour. He is also the star in the best gunfight show in town, at Old Tombstone Western Theme Park.

This is me on the trolley pointing at something out the door just before our tour begins. This was taken during the summer and I'm not wearing my jacket due to the heat (you'll have to excuse my informality).

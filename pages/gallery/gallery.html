Gallery

Guests who complete my ghost tour frequently ask me to look at photos that they took during the tour and then they will later send those photos to me. This page is my way to share those photos with the world. Tap any of the images on this page to see the full-size photo.

The Courthouse is one of the most haunted spots in Tombstone and I regularly get photos of paranormal activity here.

Boothill is very haunted and my guests often get great photos of paranormal activity here. This photo shows a few orbs over the graveyard.

Heath's Corner, at Second and Toughnut, shows a nice plasma. This plasma is still somewhat young and undefined, but it is clearly beginning to form in the background of the photo.

A vortex is the third stage of an apparition's materialization process. This photo shows a vortex at Boothill Graveyard.

Allen Street Cemetery has been open since 1884 and is Tombstone's current cemetery. Not many guests visit this site but I occasionally get a few photos from here.

The Birdcage Theatre is among the most haunted spots in Tombstone and this photo shows what appears to be the top half of a face peeking over the window ledge.

These are orbs in the street beside our city park. You can see the entrance to the OK Corral in the background.

This shows a vortex at the Courthouse. If this apparition continues through the last stage then it will materialize near where this vortex touches the ground.

A plasma is the second stage of a materialization and the bottom right corner of this photo captured a good example. Some people think that this resembles a man walking, but it's too soon in the process to know for sure.

This is a clearly-defined vortex at Boothill Graveyard. Most vortexes are somewhat vertical but this has an odd horizontal orientation.

The Good Enough Mine, like all mines in Tombstone, has a lot of paranormal activity and this photo shows some nice orbs in the mine.

This is me on the trolley pointing at something out the door just before our tour begins. This was taken during the summer and I'm not wearing my jacket due to the heat (you'll have to excuse my informality).

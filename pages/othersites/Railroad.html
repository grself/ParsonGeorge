Railroad Right-of-Way

Parallel to Toughnut Street, from First to Fifth Streets

The railroad used to enter Tombstone from the southwest at about First and Toughnut streets, run parallel to Toughnut and terminate behind the depot building, which is the Tombstone City Library today. Guests visiting Tombstone can see part of the original right-of-way because there are a few old railroad cars on short rails behind the library building.

There are a number of stories about various "ghost trains" that come to Tombstone in the middle of the night. One of the most interesting happened in the 1880's while Tombstone was still a boom town. According to the legend, a ranch hand had broken his leg when his horse threw him and he was lying in the desert just outside of town. He could hear a train but was not able to crawl to the tracks due to the pain. Just as he passed out he dreamed that the train had stopped beside him. When he woke up he was on the front porch of Dr. George Goodfellow in Tombstone but had no idea how he had gotten there. The really odd thing about this story is that the railroad did not come to Tombstone until 1903, about 20 years after this cowboy was saved by a train.

Tour Information: The trolley tour runs down Toughnut street, parallel to the railroad right-of-way, and I tell a story about the railroad as we pass by. Unfortunately, there are not many stories about the railroad in Tombstone so there is not much to tell. I included it on my website because there are occasional reports of paranormal activity here.

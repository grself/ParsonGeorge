Original City Hall

Highway 80 Between Third and Fourth Streets

This was the City Hall building from the very beginnings of Tombstone, and it is known to be haunted. Unfortunately, it was closed in about 2005 due to the damage caused by a swarm of bees that had built a hive in the wall (it is currently being restored). The building has a basement that seems to be the center of paranormal activity at this location. There are not many basements in Tombstone, but some researchers believe that basements make it easier for spirits to move between the mines and the surface, so buildings with basements are more susceptible to paranormal activity.

A research group from the University of New Mexico came to Tombstone several years before the building was closed and they got permission to stay in the building after closing time. They had their recorders and other machines running as they went down the stairs into the basement. One of them noticed that the readings on some of their machines were higher than they had ever seen before ("pegged out," he said) and it was becoming difficult to breathe. They only stayed in the basement a few minutes and then called off their investigation and left. While they were in Tombstone they stopped at several other sites, but never attempted to return to the City Hall.

Tour Information: The original city hall is on Freemont Street but our trolley does not pass that location during our tour. I included it on my website because there frequent reports of paranormal activity there.

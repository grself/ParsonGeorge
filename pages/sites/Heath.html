John Heath's Corner

Corner of Second and Toughnut Streets

On the morning of February 22, 1884, John Heath was taken from the Court House and lynched on a telegraph pole on the corner of Second and Toughnut streets and the story of his death is dramatically covered during the Ghost Tour. After he died there was a coroner's inquest, but the jury wanted to protect the mob of men who had hanged Heath, so they concluded: "We the undersigned, a jury of inquest, find that John Heath came to his death from emphysema of the lungs – a disease common in high altitudes – which might have been caused by strangulation, self-inflicted or otherwise." Since he was found at a "high altitude" (the top of a telegraph pole) and may have suffered emphysema, and, further, his death may have been "self-inflicted," no one was ever charged with his death. I cannot prove this, but it is said that John Heath's lawyer lived across the street from where he was lynched and the mob chose that corner intentionally to "send a message" to that lawyer.

Because he had died so violently and no one was ever held responsible for the murder, it is believed that his spirit frequently returns to this corner to seek justice. Guests report hearing him screaming on a pole that is no longer here. There are also numerous photos that show paranormal activity on this corner, like the two included on this page: they were taken by the same guest a few seconds apart, the top one has no paranormal activity and the other has a vortex.

Tour Information: The corner where John Heath was lynched is on our tour route and I share some of the history and paranormal activity witnessed here as we pass this location.

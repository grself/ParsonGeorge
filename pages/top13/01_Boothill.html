Boothill Graveyard

Highway 80 at the edge of Tombstone

Note: This page concerns Boothill's paranormal activity. For information about Boothill's history visit About Boothill.

This is said to be the most haunted spot in Tombstone.

Sometimes, late at night, guests report hearing a dog or coyote howling inside the Graveyard. While no one has ever actually seen such an animal there the stories are common enough that there is, no doubt, something in there making that sort of noise. Orbs, vortexes, and plasmas are all regularly photographed at Boothill and guests should take many pictures at Boothill since they never know what may show up. In fact, over the years guests have even sent me photos showing daytime paranormal activity, like shadow-men.

A number of years ago one of the guests shared a rather unusual story with me after our tour. She said that she had visited Boothill earlier in the day and felt a "pull" toward the far end of the graveyard. She followed her instinct and ended up at the grave of Johnny Blair which is by itself out just past the edge of the graveyard. Johnny died of smallpox at a small camp a few miles from Tombstone and his buddies were not willing to touch his body to bring it for burial. They threw a rope around his feet and dragged him to the graveyard behind a horse. They dug his grave, dragged him into that grave, and then threw the rope in behind him. My guest arrived at his grave and said that she felt an overwhelming urge to shout "Woop!" like a cowboy would have as he came into town. After a single shout, which must have startled her friends, she left the graveyard and had no other incidents. She figured that the spirit of Johnny Blair was making her behave that way.

Tour Information: The highlight of the tour is our stop at Boothill Graveyard where I tell several stories about the ghosts who live here. This is also the place where I tell my "feature" story, which is an old-fashioned camp-fire type of ghost story. I have many that I have written and I choose one that is appropriate for the season. Because this is the most haunted place in Tombstone I also get more guest photos from this location than anywhere else and I share several of those while we are stopped at Boothill. If you join me on the tour you will like this particular stop, but even if you cannot join me you should stop by Boothill and visit with the folks buried there.

Buford House

Safford Street Between First and Second

This is said to be the second most haunted spot in Tombstone.

The Buford house has been featured on the History Channel as one of the most haunted locations in Tombstone and that is a well-deserved reputation. It was a bed-and-breakfast for many years and the guests who stayed there often reported paranormal activity. It isn't certain why spirits would choose this particular spot, but this house seems to be more haunted than other locations around Tombstone. Perhaps there is some sort of geological artifact below this house that helps to channel spirits into this one location. Whatever the reason, the Buford House remains one of the most haunted locations in Tombstone.

One of the Ghost Tour drivers several years ago reported that his young daughter spent the night with a friend in the Buford house. She said that late that night she saw colored lights shining across the walls of her room almost like an automobile was driving by on a street outside, but she was on the side of the house away from the street. She also reported feeling very calm and peaceful while in the presence of those lights, not frightened as someone may think.

Paranormal touches seem to be rather common around the Buford house. When it was a bed-and-breakfast guests would often report feeling a cat rubbing against their legs at the breakfast table, but when they reached down to pet it there would be nothing there. Guests would also report feeling something brush against their skin as they walked across the porch in front of the Buford House. In fact, some people who walk past the Buford House on the street feel something brush past them.

Not far from the Buford House is the  location where George Daves took his own life in 1888, which I tell about during the tour.

Tour Information: The Buford house is a private residence and no longer a public bed-and-breakfast. Because of that, the tour no longer passes the house since I felt like the flash from cameras and phones was disturbing to the owners while they tried to enjoy a quiet evening on the front porch. The stories and photos, though, from years gone by are still very interesting.

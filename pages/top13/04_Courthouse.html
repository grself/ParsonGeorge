Courthouse

Corner of Third and Toughnut Streets

This is said to be the fourth most haunted spot in Tombstone.

The Courthouse was built in 1881 when Tombstone was selected to be the first county seat for Cochise County. This building held the county court and the various offices needed by leaders in those early days. Because the court met here, there was a small holding cell on the first floor for prisoners appearing before the judge that day. That cell was also the last "home" for the seven men who swung from the gallows behind the Courthouse.

It seems reasonable to assume that since this building housed so many violent criminals during their trials, and they would have been upset at the sentences handed down, their spirits come back today to seek some sort of retribution. Orbs are commonly photographed here, along with the occasional face appearing in the windows.

There is a local story about a light appearing in the small cupola at the top of the courthouse. The cupola is closed to the public because the narrow steps leading to it are not safe; however, the city leaves a small light on in that space year round. Once in a while, though, locals report seeing a second light moving around late at night in the cupola. It never seems to be doing much, just moves among the windows almost like a lantern being carried around by some night watchman inside that tiny room. Perhaps there was a time when a lantern was kept burning in what would have been the tallest structure in town as a beacon for travelers coming to Tombstone out of the surrounding mountains and some long dead county employee is still trying to show guests the way to town.

I once received an email message with several photographs of the courthouse. The writer said that he was with a small party of four visiting Tombstone the previous weekend. They had taken my tour on Friday evening and after the tour decided to walk to the courthouse. They all experienced the feeling of some presence watching them when they got near the courthouse. The photos they took that evening had a number of orbs, but the entire group went back to the courthouse the next evening and got photos of something in the window looking out at them.

Tour Information: Our trolley stops at the Courthouse and I tell several stories about the history and paranormal activity associated with this building. I spend a good bit of time telling about the lynching of John Heath and the seven men who were legally hanged on our gallows behind the courthouse.

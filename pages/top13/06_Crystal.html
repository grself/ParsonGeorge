Crystal Palace

Corner of Fifth and Allen Streets

This is said to be the sixth most haunted spot in Tombstone.

One of the men who used to work at Old Tombstone Western Theme Park reported an incident when he was invited to join a group in the basement of the Crystal Palace for a ghost hunt after closing hours several years ago. The basement of the Crystal Palace used to connect to one of the mines, but that entrance was walled shut many decades ago. It is believed, though, that spirits from the mine still use the basement of the Crystal Palace as a gathering spot before visiting above ground, so there is a good bit of paranormal activity here. When the ghost hunt began, one person in the group adjusted a large machine sitting on a table against the mine wall that he was using to detect paranormal activity. At any rate, soon after the session began, the Old Tombstone worker heard a voice that quietly whispered in his ear "Get Out." He said that he laughed at that voice, then it repeated the demand but loudly enough for the other people in the room to hear. When he did not move fast enough, he was pushed (or maybe slapped) on his back so hard that it knocked him down. He reported that when he got home a little later his wife noticed a huge hand print in the middle of his back.

Bikers tend to like our town and groups frequently visit. Several years ago, a biker got a bit "fresh" with one of the women working at the Crystal Palace. He later went to the men's room and while he was washing his hands he looked in the mirror above the sink and noticed that a man in period costume was standing behind him. The biker thought that it was a great costume so he turned around to speak to the gentlemen, but no one was there. The biker thought that was very strange and he turned back to the sink to rinse his hands. When he did, he noticed in the mirror that gentleman was now standing just behind him. This time, though, the gentleman reached forward, grabbed the biker's shoulder, and spun him around; but there was no one there. The biker quickly left the building and did not come back. We know that Virgil Earp used to keep the peace in this place using techniques similar to what this biker experienced, he would physically throw misbehaving cowboys out onto the street and tell them to never come back. While we cannot know if this biker actually ran across Virgil, something made him leave the bar and not return. Ever.

Tour Information: Our trolley stops at the corner of Fifth and Allen streets and I tell several stories about the history and paranormal activity associated with the buildings on this corner, including the Crystal Palace. This was a bloody corner of Tombstone and it was here that Virgil Earp was maimed on the night of December 28, 1881 -- his assailants were never caught, or even identified. It is believed that the paranormal activity experienced here is related to at least some of murders that took place on this corner.

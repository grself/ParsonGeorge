Schieffelin Hall

Corner of Fourth and Freemont Streets

This is said to be the ninth most haunted spot in Tombstone.

Schieffelin Hall was built in 1881 as an opera house. It was designed to seat 600 people on wooden benches and it still serves the citizens of Tombstone as a theater (though the seats are more comfortable today). It is said to be the largest adobe structure in the United States that is still in use from the 19th century.

We have a local community theater troupe and they report an apparition that they call "The Lady in Red." As the group is rehearsing a play, they occasionally see the Lady in Red enter the theater, walk around in the house looking for an empty seat, and then sit down and enjoy a play that only she can see. After about five minutes or so, she simply vanishes. Some of the local actors believe that the Lady in Red is an old actor from back in the 1800s but others think that she may have been a patron who is still coming around to watch shows. Whatever is true, she is frequently reported in the building.

A number of years ago, our City Hall building was closed and some of the city offices were temporarily located to the upstairs floor of Schieffelin Hall. They reported a spirit that seemed to be fascinated by lights. They would occasionally come to work in the morning and see that the copy machine had been used overnight, leaving a stack of blank pages in the out-box. Also, sometimes in the middle of the night the locals would report that the lights were on in one of those offices, though the staff was careful to shut everything off, including the lights, when they left for the night.

Tour Information: Our trolley stops beside Schieffelin Hall and I tell stories about this location and several other locations that are within a "stone's throw" of this spot.
